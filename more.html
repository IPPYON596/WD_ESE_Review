Global Sports Authority


sports have always been an integral part of human civilization, fostering unity, discipline, and a spirit of healthy competition.
Across the globe, organizations have been established to manage and promote various sports, ensuring that they are accessible to all
and are conducted in a fair and organized manner. These authorities not only oversee the rules and regulations of their respective sports
but also work tirelessly to nurture young talent and uphold the integrity of competitions.

In India, sports authorities such as the BCCI, Hockey India, and the Indian Olympic Association have played pivotal roles in bringing
the country onto the global stage. From cricket, which enjoys unparalleled popularity, to wrestling and shooting, which have earned the
nation numerous accolades, these organizations continue to inspire and support athletes in their journey towards excellence. Below, we
explore some of these major sports authorities, their roles, and their contributions to the sporting world.

Sports authorities play a pivotal role in shaping and promoting the spirit of sportsmanship across the globe.
The International Olympic Committee (IOC), headquartered in Lausanne, Switzerland, oversees the Olympic Movement,
organizes the Summer and Winter Olympic Games, and promotes peace through sports. In football, the Fédération Internationale
de Football Association (FIFA), based in Zürich, Switzerland, manages global football tournaments, including the prestigious
FIFA World Cup, and fosters the sport's growth worldwide. The International Cricket Council (ICC), located in Dubai, governs
cricket internationally, organizing marquee events like the Cricket World Cup and setting gameplay standards. Similarly,
Fédération Internationale de Basketball (FIBA), headquartered in Mies, Switzerland, governs basketball, hosting global events
such as the Basketball World Cup. World Athletics, with its base in Monaco, leads the world in track and field events, organizing

BCCI

The governing body for cricket in India.

www.bcci.tv

The Board of Control for Cricket in India organizes international and domestic cricket matches, promotes cricket across India, and represents the nation at ICC events. It is one of the richest cricket boards in the world.


NRI

National Rifle Association of India.

thenrai.in

The National Rifle Association of India governs shooting sports in India. It organizes national championships, supports emerging athletes, and oversees participation in international competitions like the Olympics.


Hockey India

The governing body for field hockey in India.

www.hockeyindia.org

Hockey India manages the Indian national hockey teams, organizes tournaments like the Hockey India League, and works towards the development and promotion of hockey in the country.


Wrestling Federation of India

the Organization behind wrestling in India.

wrestlingfederationofindia.org

The Wrestling Federation of India manages wrestling events like the World Championships and Olympics. It nurtures talent through training camps and competitions.


Indian Olympic Association (IOA)

India's apex sports organization.

olympic.ind.in

The IOA is responsible for India's representation at the Olympic Games and other international sports events. It governs and promotes Olympic sports in the country and collaborates with national sports federations.


International Olympic Committee

The global organizer of the Olympic Games.

olympics.com

The IOC fosters international sportsmanship and peace through sports. It organizes the Summer and Winter Olympic Games, bringing together athletes from around the world to compete at the highest level.
